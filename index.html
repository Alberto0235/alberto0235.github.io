01 About

I'm an MSc student in Electronics Engineering at Politecnico di Milano, focused on hardware design — power electronics, high-speed PCB layout, and signal integrity. My projects run the full loop: schematic capture, layout, fabrication, assembly, and bench validation, not just simulation.

I completed my B.Sc. in Electronic Engineering at the University of Bologna (Cesena campus), followed by an Erasmus exchange at Universitat Politècnica de València. I'm also a certified Cadence Student Ambassador.

Most of my bring-up and debugging happens in my home lab, equipped with oscilloscopes, multimeters, and other bench instrumentation.

I'm working toward a hardware engineering role at a major tech company, with an eye on Silicon Valley.

DRSSTC Fiber-Optic Interrupter

A galvanically isolated, hand-soldered STM32F405 control board for a Tesla-coil driver, with a hardware-only E-brake safety chain and controlled-impedance USB / FSMC / SDIO routing. Currently mid bring-up.

MCU
STM32F405
Isolation
Fiber-optic, galvanic
Safety
Hardware E-brake, firmware-independent
Stackup
4 layers, 90Ω USB / 50Ω FSMC-SDIO
EDA
KiCad

Why it matters: the safety architecture. The E-brake physically de-energizes the output and halts MCU timers even if firmware is unresponsive — a mature way to reason about a system capable of generating lethal discharges.
