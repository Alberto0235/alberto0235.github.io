USB 3.2 Gen 1 — 4-Port Hub

Validated — bring-up complete

A 6-layer, impedance-controlled USB 3.2 hub carried from schematic through fabrication, assembly, and bench validation — including full SuperSpeed enumeration and measured throughput.

  • TI TUSB8044A
  • 90Ω controlled differential impedance
  • 6-layer stackup
  • USB-C cold socket, hardware-only
  • DFM review (PCBWay)
  • 5 Gbps SuperSpeed measured

Why it matters: this is the project that closes the full loop — signal integrity analysis, a real DFM review with a manufacturer, and bench measurements (102 MB/s SuperSpeed throughput, verified power-sequencing timing) rather than a simulation stopping at layout. Also includes a Python HID-to-I2C EEPROM programming tool, written from scratch.

GaN Full-Bridge LLC Resonant Converter

Simulation — design complete, not built

Full-bridge GaN LLC resonant converter designed as the front-end stage of a 400 W photovoltaic micro-inverter — 97.88% simulated efficiency at 100 kHz with zero-voltage switching across the full input range.

  • GaN devices (EPC / GaN Systems)
  • Full-bridge LLC resonant topology
  • 100 kHz–1 MHz sweep
  • Custom C sizing tool
  • LTspice, vendor SPICE models

Why it matters: a real topology trade-off study (5 candidate topologies compared on efficiency before choosing LLC) plus a custom C tool for resonant-tank sizing — evidence of engineering judgment, not a tutorial followed end to end. Shown honestly as simulation-stage, not built hardware.

DRSSTC Fiber-Optic Interrupter

In progress — hardware assembled, bring-up underway

A galvanically isolated, hand-soldered STM32F405 control board for a Tesla-coil driver, with a hardware-only E-brake safety chain and controlled-impedance USB / FSMC / SDIO routing. Currently mid bring-up.

  • STM32F405
  • Fiber-optic galvanic isolation
  • Hardware E-brake, firmware-independent
  • 4-layer controlled impedance (90Ω USB, 50Ω FSMC/SDIO)
  • KiCad

Why it matters: the safety architecture. The E-brake physically de-energizes the output and halts MCU timers even if firmware is unresponsive — a mature way to reason about a system capable of generating lethal discharges. Shown as work in progress, not glossed over.
